With organisations across the National Health Service significantly impacted by the current pandemic, there has never been a riskier time to suffer a data breach or cyber-attack.

NHS employees are being asked to work from home and depleted workforces mean there is an increased chance of an incident occurring.

Data breaches and cyber-attacks are certainly on the rise especially now as we are moving towards an information age. NHS is introducing new information processing facilities and technologies which means more electronical records are available and accessible.

In 2017 the WannaCry ransomware offense shook the NHS which cost Â£92million and 19000 appointments were cancelled, this attack had a devastating effect on the operations and service delivery.

The threats are not always coming from cyber criminals or external sources, the biggest threat to data breaches and cyber-attacks come from internal staff due to lack of controls or awareness. Information security needs to be taken very seriously in the NHS due to the type of data it holds (personal identifiable information).

One of the biggest assets and responsibility to the NHS is the information it holds; this needs to be highly protected. Best practice for protecting information is taking a systemic approach consisting of people, processes and technology. Loss of information will lead to business disruption and service delivery.

Services Proposed

Risk Assessments and Treatment Methodology

Provide resource to develop a risk assessment and treatment methodology in line with the ISO27001 standard. Resource to also risk assess all assets and identify internal/external threats and present them to the leadership team.

27002 Controls Implementation

Conducting a gap analysis to identify existing controls or lack of controls. Provide consultancy to support the implementations of controls in accordance with the international standard ISO 27002., working with mid-level management to design processes to build an ISMS. Organisations can select a number of frameworks depending on vulnerabilities or areas which require maturity.

Independent Audits

Service available for organisations to seek independent review or feedback of how business functions are operating, the governance and awareness of security controls or compliance with the ISO27001 standard.

Awareness and Training

Provide online/remote workshops for all NHS employees and organisations, in addition to the mandatory training to make them aware of their responsibilities and how they can contribute towards protecting the information of the NHS, creating a good security culture nationally.

Management Reviews

This is a service for NHS leaders, embedding a process for the leadership team to conduct management reviews at planned intervals, which will ensure that external/internal threats have been identified and presented so that well-informed decisions can be made on the vulnerable areas.

Continual Improvement Planning

This service is to help organisations understand the importance of continual improvement and design and embed the process following the PDCA lifecycle.
